# Why do we need a caption dissection?

The FBR tone is something we are trying to establish as we're releasing more media, however for anyone except Sebastian, it could be hard to come up with a caption that sounds FBR. 
We also are going to have a Chat bot at some point which can be used to generate captions, so the more documentation we have on the tone and approach the better the AI will do with writing them. 

____ 
# Differences in our caption approach to different types of media output:

## AFBS (art for better science): 

Our running campaign. 
We want these videos to lead people to more of our informational content. The caption should reflect the type of people we want + who will be invested in the FBR activity, separate to music (at this stage we are trying to move people away from thinking FBRs main activity is events) 
The captions should teach people something, the reward of watching the AFBS videos is a return in knowledge or a new thought. 

## NRF (new reel format):

These captions are very circumstantial, since they are led by whatever clip we choose to put at the end of a CNC SF.
We can get a bit creative like in example 1 
Or a bit more straight forward like in example 2 
However the underlying common theme in these captions is humour. 
We want people to be confused by the interruption of the SF + the caption should complement it in a way that will make someone probably still a bit confused but think its funny.  
## CNC: 

With CNC we have 2 types of media formats on all platforms except for Youtube, where we post long form as well. 

Carousels: 
Carousels are used to highlight the artists sound the best parts of their set, so its very music focused and so is the caption.
We keep it simple because there isn't much of a need to write a caption besides a tag and a location. 
### Caption example: 
Bouki CNC019 @ FBR cafe  

Short forms: 
Short forms are more to highlight the artist, peoples reactions to the artist and the event. 

We came up with a new caption approach since all the caption we saw from other people who release electronic music events content was boring and useless. 
We write about the artist as a person rather than to do with music. 
This idea came about when Charlotte saw a video of silva bumpa talking about a ham wrap. 
### Strategy behind this idea:

Have a bit of a click bait approach to the caption. 
By engineering people to read the caption, they stay on the video longer meaning:
- longer view times 
- repeated views in one interaction = more views

We have been trying to decipher what ways we can express the passage of time and guide a user input interaction with time. The circular approach to a clock is classical; same as the crown, but, it presents some other limitations.  
The clocks hand based time approach and crown interaction are limited to a very rigid 12 h 60 min mental model. By breaking these constraints we actually confuse the user and make the seemingly attractive and intuitive approach very shit to use.  
If the complete circle is constantly changing its defined period, how will the user interpret that relative to a clock. So we looked at other objects and how they interact with time, how they are to use, and how long they take to learn. Sometimes the design is wrong, sometimes the system, sometimes the logic. This time we fucked the logic. So we ditched it.
