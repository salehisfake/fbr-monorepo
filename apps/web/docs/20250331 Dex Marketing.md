Am working on the site and came up with a potential marketing idea to get the site/the brand some really invested global followers is asking those who write about topics in our world (from coffee, tech to demoscene) to write or submit existing work as ‘feature pieces’ for FBRdex. These entries will have them credited, and will give the author a new place to market their work. 

They win on that end, and we win on two ends. One is we get to share more good content. Two is that these writers will be part of our community, literally and emotionally. 

That puts them in the perfect place to engage with the product. We know they'll love the product because of the type of things they obsess about in their writing.

It wouldn’t be too difficult to find writers willing to this for free if we’re looking through smaller but high quality blog pages and medium posts.

In we view the 'network' of the graph as one that exists not just within our website, but in our community as a whole ecology/community, we can draw invisible lines connecting node entries to the interpersonal network of the author who wrote the entry. That includes their digital social network, which in this case means the audience the writer has spent many years building. 

Word of mouth is king, and I image you could streamline and potentially automate the writer contact process to end up contacting like 20> really high value leads that will feel genuinely embedded in the digital community of FBR as a educational  brand. All external work can be tagged as such and therefore easily turned off/on, but I don't think there's a good reason to be precious about content ownership on FBRdex.
